Grain Specification

The Meridian Architecture

Grain lives at the Meridian—the threshold where machine intelligence manifests as human experience. By formalizing this layer, we create interfaces that are not just reactive, but truly representative of the underlying reasoning. This architecture enables seamless translation between AI thought processes and human-readable outputs, creating a universal language for intelligent systems.

Why Grain Exists

As AI systems become increasingly sophisticated, the gap between machine reasoning and human understanding grows wider. Grain bridges this divide by providing a standardized vocabulary that works across all surfaces—web interfaces, command-line tools, and agent-to-agent communication. Whether you're building a chatbot, an autonomous agent, or a collaborative AI tool, Grain ensures your interactions are precise, predictable, and universally understood.

Grain was designed with three core principles in mind: minimalism (zero dependencies, tiny footprint), precision (atomic primitives that do one thing well), and universality (works everywhere AI meets humans). This foundation enables developers to build complex AI interfaces without reinventing the interaction layer for every new application.
